# MLRO Monthly Report — Template and Instructions

This folder holds the standard monthly reporting form used by the Money
Laundering Reporting Officer of each entity within the group to discharge
the duties set out under Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 and its
implementing regulations. The template is written to be filled in by a
qualified human compliance officer. It is not a pre-drafted report and
must never be filed as if the figures it contains were generated
automatically.

## Scope of Application

The same template is used by every entity in the group. As at the date
of this folder, that list is FG LLC, FG BRANCH, MADISON LLC, GRAMALTIN AS,
NAPLES LLC and ZOE FZE. The template is reviewed at the start of each
calendar year and updated whenever a regulatory change makes a section
obsolete or incomplete.

## How to Use the Template

1. At the start of each month, the MLRO of the entity downloads a copy
   of `TEMPLATE.md` and renames it using the convention
   `<ENTITY>_MLRO_<YYYY>-<MM>.md`, for example `FG-LLC_MLRO_2026-05.md`.
2. The MLRO then fills in every field of the form, drawing on the
   underlying working papers, the goAML submissions log, the screening
   register and the customer due diligence files. Every figure must be
   traceable to a source record that can be produced on request.
3. Where a section does not apply to the entity during the reporting
   period, the MLRO writes "Not applicable during the period" rather
   than leaving the section blank, so that the reader is left in no
   doubt that the section has been considered.
4. When the form is complete, the MLRO converts the file to PDF, signs
   the signature block, obtains the counter-signature of the Chief
   Executive Officer or Managing Director, and files the signed PDF in
   the compliance archive under a file name that mirrors the source
   markdown file.
5. The signed PDF is retained for a minimum of ten years in accordance
   with Article 24 of the AML/CFT/CPF Law. A copy is made available to
   the Ministry of Economy, the Financial Intelligence Unit, and any
   other competent authority upon lawful request.
6. The Asana task associated with the reporting period is then closed,
   with the archive reference of the signed PDF recorded in the task
   comment thread.

## A Note on Accuracy and Honesty

The quality of this report is the quality of the entity's compliance
function. Inflated figures, fabricated attendance records, or optimistic
assessments of the control environment do more harm than good. Where a
weakness exists, the MLRO must say so plainly and propose a remediation
path. Where a deadline has been missed, the reason must be recorded and
the corrective action must be set out. The Ministry of Economy and the
Financial Intelligence Unit read these reports carefully, and they are
far more tolerant of an honest account of a manageable problem than they
are of a report that was later found to have concealed one.

## Regulatory Basis

The template has been drafted with reference to the following
authorities, each of which should be familiar to the MLRO and is held in
the compliance library of the entity:

- Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 on the Criminalisation of Money
  Laundering, the Financing of Terrorism and the Financing of Illegal
  Organisations, in particular Articles 12 to 14 on customer due
  diligence, Articles 20 and 21 on the duties of the Compliance
  Officer, Article 24 on record retention, Articles 26 and 27 on the
  filing of Suspicious Transaction Reports, Article 29 on the
  prohibition of tipping off, and Article 35 on targeted financial
  sanctions.
- Cabinet Resolution No. 134 of 2025 promulgating the Implementing
  Regulations, in particular Article 5 on risk appetite, Articles 7 to
  10 on customer due diligence tiers, Article 14 on politically exposed
  persons and enhanced due diligence, Article 16 on cross-border cash,
  Article 18 on notification of changes to the Compliance Officer, and
  Article 19 on the internal review cycle.
- Cabinet Resolution No. 74 of 2020 concerning Targeted Financial
  Sanctions, in particular Articles 4 to 7 on the execution of freezing
  measures within twenty-four hours, reporting to the Executive Office
  for Control and Non-Proliferation, and the filing of the Confirmed
  Name Match Report within five business days.
- Cabinet Resolution No. 156 of 2025 on Proliferation Financing and
  Dual-Use Controls.
- Cabinet Decision No. 109 of 2023 on the Beneficial Ownership
  Register, in particular the twenty-five per cent ownership threshold
  and the fifteen-working-day re-verification deadline.
- Cabinet Resolution No. 71 of 2024 on Administrative Penalties.
- Ministry of Economy Circular No. 08/AML of 2021 addressed to Dealers
  in Precious Metals and Stones, including the goAML registration
  requirement, the quarterly DPMS reporting obligation, and the
  AED 55,000 cash transaction reporting threshold.
- London Bullion Market Association Responsible Gold Guidance
  version 9, the UAE Ministry of Economy Responsible Sourcing of Gold
  framework, and the Dubai Good Delivery standard where the entity is
  engaged in gold refining or trading.
- Financial Action Task Force Recommendations 22 and 23 on dealers in
  precious metals and stones.

## Versioning

Each change to the template is recorded in the git history of this
folder. Before any change is promoted, the compliance function is to
ensure that the change is consistent with the authorities listed above
and that the effective date of the change is communicated to every MLRO
in the group.

## Why This Template Is a Form and Not a Pre-Filled Report

The MLRO monthly report is a statement of fact made by a named
individual under personal responsibility. The figures in the report must
come from the entity's own records for the period to which the report
relates. It is not permissible, and it would not be lawful, to
pre-generate a report for a period that has not yet closed, or to
populate the numerical fields from any source other than the entity's
own books and records. The template is therefore deliberately left
blank, and it is the MLRO who brings it to life each month.
