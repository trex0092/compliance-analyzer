## 3. Risk appetite (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 5)

Fine Gold LLC does not accept customers or counterparties that are on
any UN, OFAC, EU, UK, UAE or EOCN sanctions list. The firm does not
accept cash above AED 1,000,000 in a single transaction under any
circumstances. The firm does not deal with jurisdictions under FATF
counter-measures. The firm does not deal in metal of unknown
provenance. Any customer assessed as Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) is
accepted only after written senior-management approval, and in the
case of Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) only after Board approval.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every employee, officer, contractor, and agent
acting on behalf of Fine Gold LLC. It covers customer onboarding,
ongoing monitoring, transaction screening, sanctions and TFS controls,
reporting of suspicious activity, record keeping, training, and the
response to any regulatory request.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence (FDL Art.12 to Art.14, Cabinet Resolution
134/2025 Art.7 to Art.10)

Every customer is assigned a risk tier on onboarding: SDD (simplified),
CDD (standard), or EDD (enhanced). The tier is driven by the entity's
HAWKEYE STERLING risk-scoring engine using the likelihood and impact
factors set out in Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 and the firm's own
risk register. The tier determines the review cadence (12 months for
SDD, 6 months for CDD, 3 months for EDD) and the level of evidence
required. PEP status, adverse media, and sanctions proximity all raise
the tier automatically. Beneficial ownership down to 25% is verified
on every legal-entity customer (Cabinet Decision 109/2023).

## 6. Ongoing monitoring (FDL Art.13)

Every customer file is re-screened against sanctions and PEP lists at
the review cadence above, and immediately after any list update. Any
change of circumstance — new director, UBO change, change of business
— triggers a re-assessment within 15 working days (Cabinet Decision
109/2023).

## 7. Targeted Financial Sanctions (FDL Art.35, Cabinet Resolution
74/2020)

On any confirmed match, the firm executes a freeze within 24 clock
hours, reports to the Executive Office for Anti-Money Laundering and
Countering the Financing of Terrorism, and files the CNMR within 5
business days. On a potential (non-confirmed) match the MLRO decides
whether to confirm or dismiss, and documents the reasoning. Funds and
assets subject to a freeze are segregated in a way that makes
accidental release impossible.

## 8. Suspicious activity reporting (FDL Art.26 and Art.27)

Any employee who forms a reasonable suspicion of money laundering,
terrorism financing, proliferation financing, or predicate crime
escalates immediately to the MLRO through the HAWKEYE internal
channel. The MLRO decides whether to file an STR, SAR, or other
report. Filings are made through goAML using the /goaml skill to
guarantee schema compliance. The subject of a filing is never
informed of the filing or of the enquiry leading to it. This
prohibition is restated in every training session.

## 9. Tipping-off prohibition (FDL Art.29)

Tipping off a subject or a related person is a criminal offence. This
policy treats any suspected tipping-off as gross misconduct. All
internal and external communications that could reveal a filing are
restricted to the MLRO and the Compliance Officer and are logged.

## 10. Cash and threshold controls

Fine Gold LLC trades precious metals and is therefore subject to
MoE Circular 08/AML/2021. Cash transactions at or above AED 55,000
(single or aggregated over a 30-day window for the same customer)
trigger a DPMSR filing through goAML. Cross-border cash and bearer
negotiable instruments at or above AED 60,000 trigger a CNMR under
Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 16. These thresholds are
enforced automatically by the HAWKEYE STERLING application and are
not subject to discretionary override.

## 11. Responsible sourcing of gold

Fine Gold LLC is the principal LBMA-aligned entity in the group. It
applies LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance v9 and the UAE MoE Responsible
Sourcing of Gold Framework to every gold intake, including the five-
step OECD due-diligence framework, CAHRA screening, annual audit, and
public disclosure obligations. Metal from CAHRAs is subject to Enhanced
Due Diligence and Board approval.

## 12. Training (FDL Art.22)

Every employee completes AML/CFT/CPF training at onboarding and at
least once per calendar year thereafter. Sector-specific modules on
cash thresholds, DPMS responsibilities under MoE Circular 08/AML/2021,
and sanctions handling are delivered to front-office staff at least
twice per year. Training attendance and assessment results are
retained for ten years.

## 13. Record keeping (FDL Art.24)

The firm retains every relevant record for a minimum of ten years
from the date of the last transaction with the customer. The full
retention schedule is documented separately under the title "Records
Retention Schedule — Fine Gold LLC" and forms part of this policy.

## 14. Policy review (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 19)

The MLRO reviews this policy at least annually and within 30 days of
any new MoE circular, federal amendment, or FATF-level change that
affects the firm. The updated policy is presented to the Compliance
Officer and to the Board for re-approval before it takes effect.

## 15. Breach and penalty framework

Breaches of this policy are disciplinary matters and may additionally
attract administrative penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024 in
the range of AED 10,000 to AED 100,000,000 depending on severity.
Employees who act in good faith to file a report are protected and
cannot be disciplined for the filing itself.
