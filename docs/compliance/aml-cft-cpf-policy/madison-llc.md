## 3. Risk appetite

Madison LLC does not accept cash above AED 1,000,000 in a single
transaction. It does not accept any customer on UN / OFAC / EU / UK /
UAE / EOCN lists. It does not purchase old gold from a seller without
a verified identity. PEP customers are accepted only after Board
approval. High-value single transactions (defined as above AED
500,000) are subject to an additional MLRO review before issuance of
the invoice.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every store employee, counter staff member,
buyer, repair technician, contractor, and agent acting for Madison
LLC.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence

CDD is performed on every customer whose transaction (single or
aggregated) crosses the identification threshold, and on every seller
of old gold regardless of value. The SDD tier is available only for
low-value, low-risk customer relationships with no adverse media,
no PEP status, and no sanctions proximity. Standard CDD is the
default. EDD applies to PEPs, higher-risk jurisdictions, adverse
media, and unusual transaction patterns (for example, a very large
cash purchase of investment-grade items by a new customer). EDD
requires senior management approval; PEPs require Board approval.

Beneficial ownership is verified down to 25% for every legal-entity
customer (Cabinet Decision 109/2023).

## 6. Ongoing monitoring

Review cadence: 12 months for SDD, 6 months for CDD, 3 months for
EDD. A customer whose historical pattern changes materially (for
example, a significant increase in transaction size or frequency)
triggers a re-assessment.

## 7. Sanctions and TFS (Cabinet Resolution 74/2020, FDL Art.35)

Every named customer above the identification threshold is screened
against UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE / EOCN lists on onboarding and
every re-screening cycle. A confirmed match triggers a freeze within
24 clock hours, a report to the Executive Office, and a CNMR within
5 business days. Store staff are not authorised to release an item
to a customer who has triggered a potential match until the MLRO has
cleared the transaction in writing.

## 8. STR / SAR / CTR / DPMSR / CNMR

Filings are made by the firm-wide MLRO through goAML. Retail-specific
patterns that trigger filing include: a walk-in purchase of a very
large value in cash by a customer with no prior relationship;
repeated structured purchases just below the DPMS threshold; sale to
a PEP of an item whose purpose or destination appears
disproportionate. Tipping off (FDL Art.29) is prohibited.

## 9. Cash and threshold controls

AED 55,000 DPMS threshold under MoE Circular 08/AML/2021 applies at
every till. HAWKEYE STERLING blocks invoice completion until the
DPMSR workflow is started. AED 60,000 cross-border cash threshold
applies where a customer is taking goods out of the UAE.

## 10. Old-gold buy-back procedure

Every seller of old gold to Madison LLC is identified regardless of
value. Item photograph, weight, assay note, identity document and
payment method are recorded on the RF-R16 form and attached to the
transaction. A customer presenting multiple items of unusual
provenance, or pieces that could be stolen goods, is declined and the
case is escalated to the MLRO for an STR assessment. Repeat sellers
are monitored for aggregation against AML thresholds.

## 11. Responsible sourcing

For investment-grade pieces sold at retail, the firm relies on the
responsible-sourcing file held by the group's trading entities (Fine
Gold LLC, Fine Gold Branch, Gramaltin AS, Naples LLC). Madison LLC
retains a copy of the provenance record so that a customer or
inspector can trace the item back to the refiner.

## 12. Training (FDL Art.22)

Every store employee completes AML/CFT/CPF training on joining and
at least annually. Counter staff receive targeted refresher training
on: cash thresholds, sanctions handling, PEP identification, and the
RF-R16 old-gold process at least twice a year. Attendance and
assessments are retained for ten years.

## 13. Record keeping

Minimum ten-year retention under FDL Art.24, as detailed in "Records
Retention Schedule — Madison LLC". Photos, hallmarks and assay
certificates are retained at original resolution for the item
lifetime plus the AML floor.

## 14. Policy review (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 19)

Reviewed at least annually and within 30 days of any new MoE
circular or federal amendment. Changes are approved by the MLRO,
the Compliance Officer, and the Board before taking effect.

## 15. Breach and penalty framework

Breach attracts disciplinary action and may attract administrative
penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024. Good-faith reporters are
protected.
