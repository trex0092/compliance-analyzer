## 3. Risk appetite

Zoe FZE does not deal with any counterparty on UN / OFAC / EU / UK /
UAE / EOCN lists. It does not accept cash above AED 1,000,000 in a
single transaction. It does not move stock across the free-zone
perimeter without a screening clearance. It does not deal in goods
subject to Cabinet Resolution 156/2025 dual-use or strategic-goods
controls without an explicit authorisation in place.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every employee, officer, contractor, and agent
acting for Zoe FZE, including warehouse and logistics staff who handle
inbound and outbound stock through the free-zone gates.

## 5. Customer and counterparty Due Diligence

Full CDD applies to every counterparty and every stock-movement
originator and destination. The SDD tier is not available. EDD applies
automatically where a counterparty is a PEP, is in a higher-risk
jurisdiction, has a complex ownership structure, or triggers an export-
control concern under Cabinet Resolution 156/2025. EDD requires senior
management approval; PEPs require Board approval.

Beneficial ownership is verified down to 25% (Cabinet Decision
109/2023). Free-zone shareholder filings are cross-referenced with the
firm's UBO register, and discrepancies are reconciled within 15
working days.

## 6. Sanctions and TFS (Cabinet Resolution 74/2020, FDL Art.35)

Every counterparty is screened against UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE /
EOCN lists on onboarding, at the review cadence, and at every stock
movement. Confirmed match: freeze within 24 clock hours, report to
the Executive Office, CNMR within 5 business days. The free-zone
authority is notified if required by its own procedures, in parallel
with the federal filings.

## 7. Stock movement controls

Every inbound and outbound stock movement through the free-zone gate
is paired with a HAWKEYE STERLING screening event on the counterparty,
the beneficial owner, and the destination or origin. A movement
without a cleared screening cannot take place. Warehouse staff have
no authority to release stock on their own judgement.

## 8. STR / SAR / CTR / DPMSR / CNMR

Filings are made by the firm-wide MLRO through goAML. Where the
free-zone authority has its own notification requirement for
suspicious activity, the MLRO files both in parallel. Tipping off
(FDL Art.29) is prohibited; the prohibition extends to the free-zone
authority communication channel, where only the MLRO is authorised
to respond to any AML-related question.

## 9. Cash and threshold controls

AED 55,000 DPMS threshold under MoE Circular 08/AML/2021 applies to
any cash receipt. AED 60,000 cross-border cash and bearer-negotiable-
instrument threshold under Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 16
applies at the free-zone perimeter where the movement counts as a
cross-border event for UAE Customs purposes.

## 10. Proliferation financing and dual-use (Cabinet Resolution 156/2025)

Given the free-zone profile, Zoe FZE runs a PF and dual-use risk
assessment on every new counterparty and every new product line.
Strategic-goods screening is part of the standard stock-movement
control. Red flags are documented and may trigger a filing.

## 11. Training (FDL Art.22)

All employees complete AML/CFT/CPF training on joining and at least
annually. Warehouse and gate staff receive additional training on
stock-movement screening and the consequences of releasing
unscreened stock. Attendance and assessments are retained for ten
years.

## 12. Record keeping

Minimum ten-year retention under FDL Art.24, detailed in "Records
Retention Schedule — Zoe FZE". Free-zone authority filings are kept
alongside federal filings so that the entity can answer either
authority without reassembly.

## 13. Policy review

Reviewed at least annually and within 30 days of any new MoE
circular, federal amendment, or free-zone authority directive that
affects AML/CFT/CPF controls (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Art.19).

## 14. Breach and penalty framework

Breach attracts disciplinary action and may attract administrative
penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024 and any free-zone
authority penalty regime. Good-faith reporters are protected.
