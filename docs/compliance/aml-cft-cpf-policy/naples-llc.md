## 3. Risk appetite (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 5)

Naples LLC does not accept cash above AED 1,000,000 in a single
transaction under any circumstances. It does not accept any customer
or supplier on a UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE / EOCN list. It does not
accept metal of unverifiable provenance. The entity applies the
AED 55,000 DPMS CTR threshold strictly and never splits, waives, or
defers a DPMSR filing.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every employee, counter staff member,
contractor, agent, and supplier relationship of Naples LLC. It covers
onboarding, monitoring, cash-threshold handling, sanctions screening,
filings, record keeping, training, and regulatory response.

## 5. Customer and supplier Due Diligence (FDL Art.12 to Art.14)

CDD is performed on every customer and every supplier, including
walk-in suppliers of old gold. The SDD tier is not available below
the adverse-media and sanctions screen. Standard CDD applies to
established, low-risk relationships. EDD applies to PEPs, higher-risk
jurisdictions, suppliers presenting metal without clear provenance,
and customers or suppliers associated with adverse media. EDD
onboarding requires senior management approval; PEPs require Board
approval.

Beneficial ownership is verified down to 25% for every legal-entity
customer or supplier (Cabinet Decision 109/2023). For walk-in
individual suppliers of old gold, the RF-R16 old-gold verification
record is mandatory regardless of value.

## 6. Ongoing monitoring

Review cadence: 12 months for SDD, 6 months for CDD, 3 months for
EDD. Change-of-circumstance events trigger a re-assessment within
15 working days (Cabinet Decision 109/2023). The entity runs a daily
sweep for unusual cash aggregation across same-customer transactions
in the prior 30 days.

## 7. Sanctions and TFS (Cabinet Resolution 74/2020, FDL Art.35)

On confirmed match: freeze within 24 clock hours, report to the
Executive Office, CNMR within 5 business days. On potential match:
MLRO review before any release. Counter staff are explicitly trained
never to return a cash deposit or physical metal to a customer who
has triggered a potential match until the MLRO has cleared the
transaction in writing.

## 8. STR / SAR / CTR / DPMSR / CNMR (FDL Art.26, FDL Art.27)

The entity's highest-volume filings are DPMSRs under MoE Circular
08/AML/2021 at or above AED 55,000. DPMSR filings are made through
goAML via the /goaml skill. STR filings are made on a reasonable-
suspicion basis. Tipping-off (FDL Art.29) is a criminal offence and
the prohibition is reinforced in every staff training cycle and
displayed in the counter staff briefing binder.

## 9. Cash controls — mandatory procedures

a) Every cash transaction is counted twice, by two different staff,
and recorded against the customer file at the moment of receipt.
b) AED 55,000 is enforced at the till. HAWKEYE STERLING blocks
completion until the DPMSR workflow is started.
c) Aggregation: any customer whose 30-day cash total approaches
AED 55,000 is flagged automatically for MLRO review.
d) Apparent structuring (multiple smaller cash events by the same
customer, or several customers sharing an address or phone number)
is documented as a potential red flag and escalated.
e) Cash above AED 1,000,000 is not accepted under any circumstances,
even with MLRO override.

## 10. Responsible sourcing of gold

Naples LLC applies the LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance v9 and the UAE
MoE Responsible Sourcing of Gold Framework to every intake of
investment-grade metal. The five-step OECD due-diligence framework
is applied, with CAHRA screening and annual audit. Old-gold intake
from walk-in suppliers is documented separately under the RF-R16
process but is subject to the same provenance standard.

## 11. Training (FDL Art.22)

Every employee completes AML/CFT/CPF training on joining and at least
annually. Counter staff receive additional targeted training on cash
thresholds, structuring red flags, sanctions handling and the RF-R16
old-gold process twice per year. Attendance and assessments are
retained for ten years.

## 12. Record keeping

Minimum ten-year retention under FDL Art.24, detailed in "Records
Retention Schedule — Naples LLC", with a dedicated index of AED 55,000
threshold events and structuring-red-flag notes.

## 13. Policy review (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 19)

Reviewed at least annually and within 30 days of any new MoE
circular, federal amendment, or updated goAML schema from the UAE
FIU. Any update is approved by the MLRO, the Compliance Officer, and
the Board before taking effect.

## 14. Breach and penalty framework

Breach is a disciplinary matter and may attract administrative
penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024. Good-faith reporters are
protected.
