## 3. Risk appetite

Gramaltin AS does not trade with any counterparty in a jurisdiction
subject to FATF counter-measures, on any UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE /
EOCN sanctions list, or subject to UAE dual-use export controls under
Cabinet Resolution 156/2025 without an explicit exemption in place.
Every cross-border transaction is cleared against these criteria
before settlement. The entity does not accept cash settlement for
any cross-border transaction.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every employee, officer, contractor and agent
acting on behalf of Gramaltin AS. Given the cross-border profile, it
applies explicitly to correspondent-bank interactions, customs and
export filings, shipping and logistics providers, and to any
beneficial owner, director or signatory of a counterparty.

## 5. Customer and counterparty Due Diligence (FDL Art.12 to Art.14)

Counterparty CDD is full-file on every relationship. The SDD tier is
not available for Gramaltin AS counterparties. Standard CDD applies
to established counterparties in well-regulated jurisdictions with
verified UBOs. EDD applies to any counterparty that: is in a higher-
risk jurisdiction, has a complex ownership structure, is a PEP or
PEP-associated, has adverse media, or triggers an export-control
concern under Cabinet Resolution 156/2025. EDD onboarding requires
Board approval.

Beneficial ownership is verified down to 25% (Cabinet Decision
109/2023). Where the counterparty is itself a financial institution,
a correspondent-style due-diligence questionnaire is used in addition.

## 6. Sanctions screening (FDL Art.35, Cabinet Resolution 74/2020)

Every counterparty and every shipment is screened against UN, OFAC
(including secondary-sanctions screening), EU, UK, UAE and EOCN
lists. The export-control screen additionally checks strategic-goods
lists under Cabinet Resolution 156/2025. No list is skipped.
Confirmed matches trigger a freeze within 24 clock hours, a report to
the Executive Office, and a CNMR filed within 5 business days.

## 7. Wire transfers (FATF Recommendation 16)

Every cross-border wire carries full originator and beneficiary
information. The firm does not strip information from a payment
message under any circumstances. Missing information on an inbound
transfer triggers a hold and an MLRO review.

## 8. STR / SAR / CTR / DPMSR / CNMR

Reports are filed through goAML by the firm-wide MLRO. Given the
cross-border exposure, CNMR filings for strategic goods and TFS
notifications to the Executive Office occur more frequently than for
the group's domestic entities. The tipping-off prohibition (FDL
Art.29) applies without modification, including in communications
with the counterparty's bank or shipping agent.

## 9. Proliferation financing and dual-use (Cabinet Resolution 156/2025)

Gramaltin AS performs a dedicated PF and dual-use risk assessment on
every new counterparty and annually thereafter. Strategic-goods
screening is part of the standard onboarding flow and is not opt-out.
Red flags (such as a freight-forwarder in a high-risk jurisdiction
with no commercial rationale) trigger EDD and may trigger a filing.

## 10. Cash and threshold controls

Cash is not accepted for cross-border trade. The AED 60,000 cross-
border cash and bearer-negotiable-instrument threshold under Cabinet
Resolution 134/2025 Article 16 is therefore a fallback control rather
than a routine one, and any event approaching it triggers an MLRO
review. The AED 55,000 domestic DPMS threshold under MoE Circular
08/AML/2021 applies to any incidental domestic cash receipt.

## 11. Training

All employees complete AML/CFT/CPF training on joining and at least
annually (FDL Art.22). Additional, mandatory training is delivered
on: sanctions (including secondary sanctions), export controls under
Cabinet Resolution 156/2025, correspondent banking risk, and FATF
Rec. 16 wire-transfer requirements. Attendance and assessments are
retained for ten years.

## 12. Record keeping

Ten-year retention under FDL Art.24. Cross-border evidence (bills of
lading, customs, SWIFT, screening outcomes, risk-assessment notes) is
held as a single bundle per transaction, as detailed in "Records
Retention Schedule — Gramaltin AS".

## 13. Policy review

Reviewed at least annually and within 30 days of any change in UAE
law, any FATF update of the high-risk and monitored jurisdictions
list, or any substantive change in a counterparty jurisdiction's
AML/CFT/CPF regime that affects the firm (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025
Art.19).

## 14. Breach and penalty framework

Breach is a disciplinary matter and may attract administrative
penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024 and, where cross-border
sanctions are implicated, criminal liability under the underlying
federal decree-laws. Good-faith reporters are protected.
