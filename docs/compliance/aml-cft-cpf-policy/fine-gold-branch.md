Fine Gold Branch does not hold separate legal personality. It operates
under the parent's trade licence and within the parent's AML/CFT/CPF
programme. This policy is issued so that Branch-specific duties and
escalation paths are documented in one place, not so that the Branch
operates as a distinct regulated entity.

Primary MLRO: Luisa Fernanda (firm-wide).

Reporting line from the Branch: Branch CO escalates to the firm-wide

## 3. Risk appetite

The Branch applies the parent's risk appetite without variation.
No local discretion is permitted to widen the appetite, to accept a
customer otherwise excluded, to exceed a cash threshold, or to waive
EDD. Any proposed narrowing of the appetite is permitted on local
grounds (for example, a local market concern) with a written note
from the Branch CO countersigned by the firm-wide MLRO.

## 4. Scope

This policy applies to every employee of the Branch and to any
contractor or agent acting on its behalf. It covers onboarding,
monitoring, screening, sanctions and TFS controls, reporting, record
keeping, training, and response to regulatory requests.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence

CDD is performed under the same tiers as the parent: SDD, CDD, EDD.
The HAWKEYE STERLING tenant is shared with the parent so that a
customer common to both entities has a single, consistent risk
assessment. Branch staff cannot create a parallel file in a private
system. Beneficial ownership down to 25% is verified on every
legal-entity customer (Cabinet Decision 109/2023).

## 6. Ongoing monitoring

Monitoring cadence matches the parent (12 / 6 / 3 months for SDD /
CDD / EDD). Any change of circumstance (director, UBO, business
model) triggers a re-assessment within 15 working days (Cabinet
Decision 109/2023). Branch CO is accountable for delivering the
re-assessment; firm-wide MLRO signs it off.

## 7. Sanctions and TFS (Cabinet Resolution 74/2020)

Confirmed matches: freeze within 24 clock hours, report to the
Executive Office, file CNMR within 5 business days. On a potential
match the Branch CO notifies the firm-wide MLRO immediately and
awaits the MLRO's written direction. No local disposal of a potential
match is permitted.

## 8. STR / SAR / CTR / DPMSR / CNMR

Escalation from the Branch to the firm-wide MLRO is mandatory. The
Branch does not file reports in its own name; filings are made by the
firm-wide MLRO through goAML on behalf of Fine Gold LLC, with the
Branch's involvement recorded in the filing narrative. The tipping-
off prohibition (FDL Art.29) applies without modification.

## 9. Cash and threshold controls

MoE Circular 08/AML/2021 applies in full. The AED 55,000 DPMS cash
reporting threshold and the AED 60,000 cross-border threshold are
enforced at Branch tills by the HAWKEYE STERLING application. Branch
staff are explicitly forbidden to split a transaction to avoid a
threshold; any suspected split is escalated as a potential structuring
red flag.

## 10. Responsible sourcing of gold

Where the Branch sources or receives gold in its own right, it
applies the LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance v9 and the UAE MoE
Responsible Sourcing of Gold Framework. Where it sells or distributes
parent-sourced metal, it relies on the parent's due-diligence file
and does not re-perform the five-step check, but it retains a copy
of the parent's file at Branch level.

## 11. Training

Every Branch employee completes AML/CFT/CPF training on joining and
at least once per calendar year (FDL Art.22). Because the Branch is
customer-facing, front-office staff receive an additional short
refresher on cash thresholds and sanctions handling every six months.
Attendance and assessment results are forwarded to the firm-wide
MLRO and retained for ten years.

## 12. Record keeping

Records are kept for a minimum of ten years (FDL Art.24) and are
treated as a distinct Branch record set, as explained in "Records
Retention Schedule — Fine Gold Branch".

## 13. Policy review

This policy is reviewed at least annually and within 30 days of any
new MoE circular or federal amendment (Cabinet Resolution 134/2025
Art.19). The review sits inside the parent's firm-wide review cycle
so that the Branch policy does not drift out of line with the
parent's policy.

## 14. Breach and penalty framework

Breaches are disciplinary matters and may attract administrative
penalties under Cabinet Resolution 71/2024. Branch staff who escalate
a suspicion in good faith are protected.
