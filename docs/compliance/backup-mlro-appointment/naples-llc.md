## 2. Reason for appointment

Naples LLC generates the largest share of the group's cash DPMS
transactions. The AED 55,000 CTR threshold under MoE Circular
08/AML/2021 is triggered at the till on a routine basis. Any
unavailability of the MLRO during trading hours creates real risk
that a DPMSR filing would be late or that counter staff would be
left without authoritative direction on a potential sanctions match.
The Backup MLRO is appointed to eliminate that gap.

Legal basis: FDL No. 10 of 2025 Article 20; Cabinet Resolution
134/2025 Articles 18 and 19.

## 4. Scope of authority during activation

When active, the Backup MLRO has authority to:

a) Receive suspicion reports and decide on filing.
b) File STR, SAR, CTR, DPMSR, CNMR through goAML.
c) Execute a freeze within 24 clock hours under Cabinet Resolution
74/2020.
d) Notify the Executive Office for AML/CFT.
e) Approve or decline EDD onboarding of a customer or supplier.
f) Halt a cash transaction at the till.
g) Authorise counter staff to release a deposit or return an item
only when a potential sanctions match has been formally cleared in
writing by the Backup MLRO.

## 5. Activation triggers

As for Fine Gold LLC, with an added specific trigger: during periods
of unusually high trading volume (for example, seasonal peaks or
market-price-driven surges) the primary MLRO may formally delegate
cash-threshold and RF-R16 decisions to the Backup for a defined
window, documented in writing and retained in the audit log.

## 6. Deactivation

Automatic on resolution of the trigger. Handover note covers every
filing, every cash-threshold decision, and every RF-R16 decision
taken during the active period.

## 7. Reporting and handover

Retained for ten years under FDL Art.24. Cash-threshold decisions
made by the Backup are indexed alongside the primary MLRO's
decisions in the AED 55,000 threshold register so there is no gap in
the series.

## 8. Regulatory notification

MoE notification under Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18 within
30 days of Board signature.
