## 2. Reason for appointment

Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 Article 20 requires a DPMS to have
an MLRO in place at all times. The firm's AML/CFT/CPF programme
cannot pause while the primary MLRO is unavailable. Cabinet
Resolution 134/2025 Article 18 requires the firm to notify the
Ministry of Economy of any change in its MLRO arrangements. This
appointment satisfies both points by establishing a named backup who
steps in automatically on the triggers listed below.

## 4. Scope of authority during activation

When this appointment is active, the Backup MLRO has full authority
to:

a) Receive internal suspicion reports and decide on filing.
b) File STR, SAR, CTR, DPMSR and CNMR reports through goAML on behalf
of Fine Gold LLC.
c) Execute a freeze within 24 clock hours under Cabinet Resolution
74/2020 on a confirmed sanctions match.
d) Notify the Executive Office for AML/CFT.
e) Approve or decline EDD onboarding in line with the policy.
f) Direct any employee to halt a transaction pending review.
g) Respond to a regulatory request on behalf of the firm.

The appointee does not hold authority to change this policy, to
change the firm's risk appetite, or to remove the primary MLRO from
any record.

## 5. Activation triggers

This appointment activates automatically when any one of the
following occurs:

1. The primary MLRO notifies the Board in writing that she is
unavailable for a defined period (annual leave, study leave,
planned absence).
2. The primary MLRO is unreachable for more than 8 clock hours during
a regulatory deadline (for example, the 24-hour freeze window
under Cabinet Resolution 74/2020).
3. The primary MLRO is medically unfit to perform duties, confirmed
by a written medical note.
4. The primary MLRO resigns, is dismissed, or ceases to act for any
reason. In this case the Backup MLRO acts as interim MLRO until
the Board appoints a permanent replacement and notifies the MoE
under Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18.
5. A conflict of interest on a specific matter makes it improper for
the primary MLRO to act; in that case the activation is
case-specific.

## 6. Deactivation

The appointment deactivates automatically when the activation trigger
is resolved (primary MLRO returns, medical note expires, conflict is
lifted). Any filing or decision made by the Backup MLRO during an
active period remains valid and is not reopened on deactivation.

## 7. Reporting and handover

Each activation generates a written handover note, signed by both
MLROs when the primary returns. The handover records every decision
taken, every filing made, every freeze executed, and every open
matter. The note is retained for ten years under FDL Art.24.

## 8. Regulatory notification

Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18 requires the MoE to be
notified of the identity of the Backup MLRO. The Compliance Officer
files that notification within 30 days of Board signature and
retains the receipt.
