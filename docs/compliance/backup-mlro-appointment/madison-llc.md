## 2. Reason for appointment

Madison LLC runs the group's retail jewellery outlets. Customer-
facing decisions must be answered in minutes, not days: a potential
sanctions match at the till, an RF-R16 old-gold intake from a
walk-in seller, a PEP walking into the store. If the primary MLRO
is not reachable, the store cannot be left without authority.

Legal basis: FDL No. 10 of 2025 Article 20; Cabinet Resolution
134/2025 Articles 18 and 19.

## 4. Scope of authority during activation

When active, the Backup MLRO has authority to:

a) Receive suspicion reports from store staff and decide on filing.
b) File STR, SAR, CTR, DPMSR, CNMR through goAML.
c) Execute a freeze within 24 clock hours on a confirmed sanctions
match under Cabinet Resolution 74/2020.
d) Notify the Executive Office for AML/CFT.
e) Approve or decline EDD onboarding and PEP handling.
f) Halt a till transaction, direct counter staff on a potential
match, and authorise release of an item or a deposit only after a
formal written clearance.
g) Direct the RF-R16 old-gold decision to accept or decline.

## 5. Activation triggers

As for Fine Gold LLC. An additional specific trigger: on any public
holiday or weekend when stores remain open and the primary MLRO is
not on duty, the Backup covers the stores. The primary MLRO's roster
is published to store managers one week in advance.

## 6. Deactivation

Automatic on resolution. Handover note covers every filing, every
till decision, every RF-R16 decision, and every PEP or high-value
customer interaction during the active period.

## 7. Reporting and handover

Retained for ten years under FDL Art.24. Handover note is signed by
both MLROs and by the retail compliance officer to acknowledge the
store-level consequences of decisions taken in the active period.

## 8. Regulatory notification

Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18 notification to the MoE
within 30 days of Board signature.
