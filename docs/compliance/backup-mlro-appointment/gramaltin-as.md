## 2. Reason for appointment

Gramaltin AS is the group's cross-border trading counterparty entity.
Its risk profile is concentrated in sanctions (including secondary
sanctions), correspondent banking, and dual-use controls under Cabinet
Resolution 156/2025. The regulatory deadlines that apply to those
risks (in particular the 24-hour freeze window under Cabinet
Resolution 74/2020) do not tolerate an MLRO being unreachable. The
Backup MLRO must therefore be able to step in without handover delay.

Legal basis: FDL No. 10 of 2025 Article 20 (requirement to have an
MLRO available), Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18 (notification
of changes in MLRO arrangements), Cabinet Resolution 134/2025
Article 19 (annual review).

## 4. Scope of authority during activation

When this appointment is active, the Backup MLRO has full authority
to:

a) Receive suspicion reports and decide on filing.
b) File STR, SAR, CTR, DPMSR, CNMR reports through goAML.
c) Execute a freeze within 24 clock hours on a confirmed sanctions
match under Cabinet Resolution 74/2020.
d) Notify the Executive Office for AML/CFT.
e) Respond, on behalf of the firm, to a cross-border sanctions or
export-control enquiry from a foreign regulator, in coordination
with UAE authorities.
f) Approve or decline EDD onboarding of a counterparty and approve or
decline a specific shipment.
g) Direct any employee to halt a transaction pending review.

The appointee does not have authority to change this policy or the
firm's risk appetite.

## 5. Activation triggers

As for Fine Gold LLC, with one additional specific trigger: if the
primary MLRO has an actual or perceived conflict of interest on a
specific counterparty or transaction (for example, a prior
relationship, or a reported concern raised by a foreign regulator),
the Backup acts on that matter only, while the primary continues on
all other matters.

## 6. Deactivation

Automatic on trigger resolution. Handover note covers every filing,
every freeze, every EDD decision and every regulatory communication
during the active period.

## 7. Reporting and handover

Retained for ten years under FDL Art.24. Includes a dedicated section
for any cross-border enquiry responded to during the active period,
so that the firm's position on an enquiry is documented by the
individual who responded at the time.

## 8. Regulatory notification

Notification to the MoE under Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 18
within 30 days of Board signature. Where required, the firm also
notifies a correspondent bank of the identity of the acting MLRO so
that a sanctions enquiry is routed correctly.
