## 1. Purpose of this log

This log records the MLRO's consideration of any matter that could
have given rise to a Suspicious Transaction Report, Suspicious
Activity Report, or related report during the reporting period. It
is kept month by month so the firm can demonstrate that the STR
reporting obligation has been actively discharged, not only when a
report was filed.

## 2. Regulatory basis

Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025, Articles 15 and 16, require the
MLRO to consider any matter that could raise reasonable suspicion of
money laundering, terrorism financing, proliferation financing, or
predicate crime. Articles 26 and 27 govern the filing of STRs, SARs,
and related reports through the UAE Financial Intelligence Unit
(goAML). Article 29 prohibits tipping off the subject of any filing.
Article 24 sets the ten-year record retention floor that applies to
this log and to every entry in it, nil or otherwise.

## 3. Entries for the reporting period

No entries. No matter was escalated to the MLRO during April 2026
that in her judgement met the threshold for filing under FDL
Articles 26 and 27. No matter was considered and dismissed after
reaching the threshold. No third party raised a concern that
survived initial review.

The MLRO confirms that this nil outcome is not the result of a failure
to consider: transaction monitoring inside HAWKEYE STERLING ran as
scheduled, front-office staff completed their normal escalation
routine, and the MLRO reviewed the weekly aggregation report each
week of the reporting period.

## 4. Nil declaration

"No suspicious activity was identified or considered by the MLRO
during the month of April 2026. This nil declaration confirms the
STR reporting obligation under Articles 15 and 16 of UAE Federal
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 was actively reviewed and discharged for
Fine Gold LLC."

## 5. Controls exercised during the reporting period

The following controls ran and produced no matter requiring
escalation:

a) HAWKEYE STERLING transaction monitoring on every recorded
transaction, with red flags for structuring, unusual settlement
patterns, new-customer high-value cash, and jurisdictional risk.
b) Sanctions screening on every named counterparty and every
beneficial owner on onboarding and at every review cycle
(UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE / EOCN).
c) PEP and adverse-media screen on every named customer on
onboarding and at review cadence.
d) Weekly MLRO review of the aggregation report for cash events
approaching MoE Circular 08/AML/2021 thresholds.
e) Staff escalation channel operational throughout the period.

## 6. Retention

This log and its supporting data are retained for ten years under
FDL Article 24. Nil returns are retained on the same footing as
returns that include entries.

## 7. Tipping-off reminder (FDL Article 29)

The tipping-off prohibition applies to this log. The existence of a
filing (or the absence of one) is not disclosed to any subject,
their representatives, or to any third party other than the MLRO,
the Compliance Officer, the Board, and the UAE FIU.
