## 1. Purpose of this log

This log records the MLRO's consideration of any matter that could
have given rise to a STR, SAR, CNMR, or related filing during the
reporting period in respect of Gramaltin AS. Because Gramaltin AS
operates cross-border, the log also captures consideration of
proliferation-financing and dual-use matters under Cabinet Resolution
156/2025 even if they did not ultimately result in a filing.

## 2. Regulatory basis

Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025, Articles 15 and 16 (consideration),
26 and 27 (filing), 29 (tipping off), 24 (retention), 35 (TFS).
Cabinet Resolution 74/2020 (TFS freeze and reporting timelines).
Cabinet Resolution 156/2025 (proliferation financing and dual-use).
Cabinet Resolution 134/2025 Article 16 (cross-border cash threshold
AED 60,000) and Article 19 (annual internal review). FATF
Recommendation 16 (wire-transfer information requirements).

## 3. Entries for the reporting period

No entries. No cross-border transaction, correspondent-bank
interaction, or counterparty relationship considered during April
2026 met the threshold for a STR, SAR, CNMR, or dual-use filing in
the MLRO's judgement. No counterparty triggered a confirmed
sanctions match requiring freeze under Cabinet Resolution 74/2020.
No wire-transfer information deficiency rose to the level of
suspicion.

The MLRO confirms this nil outcome was reached after running the
normal cross-border control cycle, not in the absence of review.

## 4. Nil declaration

"No suspicious activity was identified or considered by the MLRO in
respect of Gramaltin AS during the month of April 2026. This nil
declaration confirms the STR reporting obligation under Articles 15

## 5. Controls exercised during the reporting period

a) Full-list sanctions screening on every counterparty and every
shipment (UN / OFAC including secondary sanctions exposure / EU /
UK / UAE / EOCN).
b) Strategic-goods and dual-use screening under Cabinet Resolution
156/2025 on every new counterparty and every product line.
c) Wire-transfer information checks against FATF Recommendation 16
on every inbound and outbound cross-border payment.
d) AED 60,000 cross-border cash and BNI threshold monitoring (no
cash settlement was accepted during the period).
e) Weekly MLRO review of correspondent-bank communications archive.

record per the entity's retention schedule.
