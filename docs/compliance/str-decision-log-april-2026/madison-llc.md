## 1. Purpose of this log

This log records the MLRO's consideration of any matter originating
at Madison LLC retail outlets that could have given rise to a STR,
SAR, or related filing during April 2026. Retail activity brings
specific suspicion triggers: very-high-value cash purchases by new
customers, repeated structured purchases just below the DPMS
threshold, PEPs at point of sale, and unusual old-gold buy-back
patterns. The log captures consideration of each.

## 2. Regulatory basis

Federal Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025, Articles 15 and 16 (consideration),
26 and 27 (filing), 29 (tipping off), 24 (retention). MoE Circular
08/AML/2021 (DPMS cash reporting threshold AED 55,000). Cabinet
Resolution 134/2025 Article 19 (internal review).

## 3. Entries for the reporting period

No entries. No retail transaction, old-gold intake, or repair
interaction during April 2026 escalated to the MLRO met the
threshold for a STR or SAR filing in her judgement. Structured-
purchase red flags were reviewed weekly and no confirmed case was
identified during the period. No walk-in old-gold intake raised a
stolen-goods or provenance concern that escalated to a filing.

## 4. Nil declaration

"No suspicious activity was identified or considered by the MLRO in
respect of Madison LLC during the month of April 2026 that met the
threshold for a STR or SAR filing. This nil declaration confirms the
STR reporting obligation under Articles 15 and 16 of UAE Federal
Decree-Law No. 10 of 2025 was actively reviewed and discharged for
Madison LLC retail outlets."

## 5. Controls exercised during the reporting period

a) AED 55,000 DPMS threshold enforcement at every till via HAWKEYE
STERLING; structuring aggregation reviewed weekly.
b) Full-list sanctions screening (UN / OFAC / EU / UK / UAE / EOCN)
on every named customer above the identification threshold and on
every old-gold seller.
c) PEP and adverse-media screen on identified customers.
d) RF-R16 old-gold intake controls on every walk-in seller,
regardless of value.
e) Store manager escalation channel tested and staffed throughout.
f) April staff training refresher delivered on PEP identification,
cash thresholds, sanctions handling, and the RF-R16 process.

## 6. Retention

Retained for ten years under FDL Article 24 as a Madison LLC record
per the entity's retention schedule.

## 7. Tipping-off reminder (FDL Article 29)

Counter staff were reminded during the April training refresher
that any AML-related enquiry from a customer or third party is
routed to the MLRO without substantive response. Store managers
were briefed that the tipping-off prohibition extends to informal
communication (for example, suggesting to a declined old-gold
seller why the intake was not accepted).
